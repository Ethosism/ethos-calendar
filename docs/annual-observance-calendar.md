# Annual Observance Calendar

This is the annual event cycle of the Ethos Calendar.

It is closer to a religious or civilizational calendar than a handbook: named seasons, high observances, monthly reviews, and recurring practices that return every year.

The dates use the Gregorian civil calendar for public usability. Local groups may move an observance within seven days for work, climate, safety, illness, or household need.

## Boundaries

These observances are not sacraments, loyalty tests, or claims of spiritual authority.

No observance may require public confession. No observance replaces medical, legal, therapeutic, or emergency processes. Ethra phrases may be used as hinges, but Ethra fluency is never required.

## Seasons

| Dates | Season | Question |
|---|---|---|
| Jan 1 - Feb 14 | Season of Inheritance | What has been received, unfinished, damaged, or entrusted? |
| Feb 15 - Mar 31 | Season of Formation | What habits, skills, disciplines, and examples should be formed? |
| Apr 1 - May 15 | Season of Provision | What must be built, stored, funded, repaired, or shared? |
| May 16 - Jun 30 | Season of Courage | What truth, duty, or protection requires risk? |
| Jul 1 - Aug 15 | Season of Mercy | What accountability can become repair rather than mere punishment? |
| Aug 16 - Oct 31 | Season of Mortality | What ends, what must be grieved, and what should be put in order? |
| Nov 1 - Dec 31 | Season of Renewal | What should begin again after review, repair, and rest? |

## Festivals

Festivals are embodied observances: gathering, food, symbols, children, public action, and beauty. They are not only review prompts.

| Date | Festival | Tone | Core customs |
|---|---|---|---|
| Jan 1 | Festival of First Truth | Solemn morning, celebratory meal | Light one lamp, write the first fact/duty/repair, share a meal. |
| Jan 1 - Jan 7 | Vow Week | Solemn arc | Review vows, mark keep/repair/revise/release, clarify one commitment. |
| Weekend nearest Mar equinox | Festival of Formation | Bright, disciplined, intergenerational | Teach a skill, honor a mentor, give children a small responsibility. |
| Weekend nearest Jun solstice | Commons Festival | Public, useful, joyful | Repair or maintain something shared, then eat together. |
| Aug 15 | Festival of Mercy And Repair | Restorative, careful, hopeful | Begin restitution or repair while keeping necessary boundaries. |
| Weekend nearest Sep equinox | Festival of Measure | Harvest, accounting, gratitude | Review provision, debts, promises, and share surplus where possible. |
| Oct 31 - Nov 1 | Remembrance Tide | Solemn, tender, memory-bearing | Hold a mortality vigil, speak names, preserve one memory. |
| Dec 1 | Festival of Future Generations | Child-honoring, long-view, protective | Name duties owed forward and repair or fund something that outlasts the year. |
| Dec solstice | Long Night | Quiet, candlelit, reflective | Turn down light, read the year record, tell stories, rest. |
| Dec 31 | Closing of the Record | Honest, spare, preparatory | Record kept/failed/repaired/carried-forward and prepare the next first page. |

## Monthly Observances

| Date rule | Observance | Practice |
|---|---|---|
| First Sunday | Truth Audit | Review one claim, belief, plan, or story against evidence. |
| Second Sunday | Household Review | Clarify shared duties, maintenance, care burdens, and neglected repairs. |
| Third Sunday | Repair Day | Start or complete one apology, replacement, repayment, cleanup, or structural correction. |
| Fourth Sunday | Stewardship Review | Review money, time, body, tools, commitments, and future obligations. |
| Last day of month | Record Close | Write what should be carried into the next month. |

## Weekly Cycle

Every week keeps the starter rhythm:

1. Attention.
2. Duty.
3. Repair.
4. Stewardship.
5. Service.
6. Learning.
7. Review.

## Use

An individual can observe the calendar privately. A household can use it for review and repair. A group can use it for reading, service, and memory. An institution can use it for risk, stewardship, and accountability cycles.

The calendar is working when recurrence produces clearer truth, more reliable conduct, real repair, stronger memory, and better care for future consequence.
