# Daily And Weekly Rule

The daily and weekly rules are the smallest usable form of the Ethos Calendar.

They should remain light enough for ordinary life and concrete enough to change conduct.

## Daily Rule

Each day asks for three things.

1. Attention.
   Name one real fact that should not be avoided.

2. Duty.
   Choose one bounded duty that can actually be carried today.

3. Record.
   Write down one thing worth remembering or correcting.

The daily rule is not a productivity system. It does not ask for maximal output. It asks for truthful contact with reality, one real responsibility, and one act of memory.

## Weekly Rule

The week gives a broader rhythm.

| Day | Theme | Question |
|---|---|---|
| Attention | Seeing reality | What did reality ask me to see? |
| Duty | Naming responsibility | What responsibility is mine? |
| Repair | Addressing harm | What needs apology, correction, replacement, repayment, or cleanup? |
| Stewardship | Caring for what is entrusted | What resource, body, home, tool, money, or trust needs care? |
| Service | Useful help | Who can be helped concretely without performance or control? |
| Learning | Correction and growth | What knowledge, skill, or correction should be added? |
| Review | Memory and adjustment | What should continue, stop, be repaired, or be remembered next week? |

The weekly rule can be used privately, in a household, or in a small group. Group use should avoid forced confession. A participant may work with a private case without exposing details.

## Ethra Hinges

Ethra can name the movement compactly:

- `Ke rah na.` See me.
- `Na vel dev se.` I choose this duty.
- `Na dov tar mo mik.` I vow to repair what was broken.
- `Lem ga naf bi mar.` The people survive by remembering.

These are optional hinges, not participation requirements.
